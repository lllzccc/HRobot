# Product

## Register

product

## Users

HRBP, talent review facilitators, and authorized HR operators use this local web tool during talent review, nine-box calibration, reporting, and HR data preparation workflows. They need dense, readable screens that preserve the underlying HR source data and make calibration decisions traceable.

## Product Purpose

This product consolidates talent review results, people profiles, permission-scoped HR data, AI-assisted Q&A, report generation, and local packaging into one standalone HR workbench. Success means HR users can import approved source data, review and calibrate talent decisions, generate evidence-based reports, and export results without accidental data loss or workflow ambiguity.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and work-focused. The interface should feel like an internal professional tool: clear enough for repeated daily use, restrained enough for sensitive HR workflows, and polished enough to build trust in generated analysis.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style landing pages, decorative dashboards, oversized hero typography, inconsistent button treatments, and casual visual flourishes that distract from HR review work. Avoid UI changes that make reports, permissions, generated materials, or source data feel disposable.

## Design Principles

- Preserve HR workflow meaning before changing presentation.
- Use consistent component vocabulary across pages, especially titles, buttons, inputs, cards, and status text.
- Keep hierarchy compact and scannable for data-heavy review sessions.
- Make generated and imported assets visibly traceable without overwhelming the main task.
- Prefer restrained brand expression over decorative visual treatment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for readable contrast, stable layouts, visible focus states, keyboard-accessible controls, and reduced-motion-friendly interactions. Chinese HR terminology and long employee or report names must wrap or truncate predictably without breaking layouts.
